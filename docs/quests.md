## Quest System Overview

### How Quests Work

Quests in DAGMod provide structured goals and meaningful rewards as you progress through the game. The quest system features:

- **4 difficulty tiers** - Novice ‚Üí Apprentice ‚Üí Expert ‚Üí Master
- **64 total quests** - Including 40 epic race-specific quests
- **Level requirements** - Unlock higher tiers at levels 5, 15, and 25
- **Massive XP rewards** - 200 to 2500 XP per quest
- **Progressive difficulty** - Unlock higher tiers by completing lower ones
- **Persistent progress** - Quest completion saves across sessions
- **Epic storylines** - 4 race-specific quest chains (10 quests each)

### Getting Started

1. Locate **Quest Blocks** (in Hall of Champions or throughout your world)
2. Interact with Quest Block to view available quests
3. Accept quests appropriate to your current tier and level
4. Complete quest objectives (gathering items, defeating mobs, exploration)
5. Return to Quest Block to claim rewards
6. Progress to the next tier when you meet level requirements

## Quest Tier System (4 Tiers)

### Tier Progression

You must progress through tiers in order, unlocking each new tier as you level up:

**Novice (Level 1)** ‚Üí **Apprentice (Level 5)** ‚Üí **Expert (Level 15)** ‚Üí **Master (Level 25)**

Each tier has its own set of quests with increasing difficulty and rewards. You cannot accept higher-tier quests until you've reached the required level.

### Quest Tier Requirements

| Tier | Level Required | XP Reward per Quest | Difficulty |
|------|----------------|---------------------|------------|
| Novice | Level 1 | 200 XP | Tutorial/Beginner |
| Apprentice | Level 5 | 500 XP | Easy |
| Expert | Level 15 | 1500 XP | Hard |
| Master | Level 25 | 2500 XP | Very Hard |

**Note:** These are standard quest rewards. Race-specific quest chain rewards match these values for their respective tiers.

### Level Gating

Quests are **level-gated** to ensure proper progression:
- **Novice quests:** Available at level 1 (start of game)
- **Apprentice quests:** Unlock at level 5
- **Expert quests:** Unlock at level 15
- **Master quests:** Unlock at level 25

If you try to accept a quest above your level, you'll receive a clear message indicating the level requirement.

## Quest Types

Quests in DAGMod are categorized to help players understand their purpose and progression. Within these categories, quests can have various objectives, such as collecting items (Fetch Quests) or defeating enemies (Kill Quests).

### Quest Categories

-   **Main Quests**: Core story and progression quests that guide players through the main narrative of DAGMod.
-   **Side Quests**: Optional quests that offer additional lore, rewards, and challenges.
-   **Class Quests**: Specific quests tailored to each class (Warrior, Mage, Rogue), often involving unique challenges or rewards relevant to their abilities.
-   **Daily Quests**: Repeatable quests that reset daily, providing a consistent source of rewards and experience.
-   **Job Quests**: Quests typically found on job boards, involving gathering, crafting, or other tasks.

### Fetch Quests

**Objective:** Collect and deliver specific items

**How They Work:**
- Gather required items through mining, farming, crafting, or trading
- Items must be in your inventory
- Quest system checks inventory on completion
- Items are consumed when quest is completed
- Can be completed passively while playing

**Examples:**
- Gather Wood - Collect oak logs
- Mining Basics - Collect cobblestone
- Iron Harvest - Collect iron ore
- Resource gathering for race quest chains

### Kill Quests

**Objective:** Defeat specific enemies

**How They Work:**
- Hunt down and defeat the specified mobs
- Progress tracked automatically on mob death
- Must be player kills (environmental deaths don't count)
- Progress persists across game sessions
- More challenging than fetch quests

**Examples:**
- Zombie Slayer - Kill zombies
- Spider Exterminator - Kill spiders
- Boss challenges in race quest chains
- Combat trials for class progression

## Quest Strategies

### Leveling Strategy

**Optimal Quest Progression:**
1. **Levels 1-5:** Focus on Novice quests (200 XP each) + mining/combat
2. **Levels 5-15:** Complete Apprentice quests (500 XP each) + start race chain
3. **Levels 15-25:** Focus on Expert quests (1500 XP each)
4. **Levels 25+:** Complete Master quests (2500 XP each) + finish race chain

**XP Optimization:**
- Do quests while naturally playing (passive completion)
- Save high-XP quests for when you're close to leveling
- Complete race chain throughout progression (mixed tier levels)
- Combine quest objectives when possible

### Race-Specific Advantages

**Dwarves:**
- Excel at mining fetch quests (ore gathering bonuses)
- "The Forgemaster's Legacy" synergizes with mining playstyle

**Elves:**
- Efficient at wood and nature gathering quests
- Movement speed helps with exploration quests
- "Guardian of the Wilds" fits exploration playstyle

**Orcs:**
- Animal hunting quests yield more meat (bonus drops)
- Combat quests benefit from damage bonuses
- "Path of the Warlord" perfect for combat focus

**Humans:**
- +25% XP gain applies to quest rewards (HUGE)
- Most efficient leveling through quests
- "Jack of All Trades" covers all quest types

### Class-Specific Advantages

**Warriors:**
- Excel at kill quests (high HP, damage, 6 abilities)
- Can take on dangerous mobs earlier
- Battle Shout provides healing during long quest sessions

**Mages:**
- Efficient at gathering due to enchanting bonuses
- Crowd control abilities (Time Warp, Mana Burst) help with mob quests
- Arcane Barrier provides safety during dangerous quests

**Rogues:**
- Fast movement speed (+30%) speeds up fetch quests
- 7 abilities provide tools for any situation
- Vanish escapes dangerous situations
- Critical hits and backstabs help with kill quests

## Quest Management

### Accepting Quests

1. Find a Quest Block
2. Interact with the block
3. View available quests for your tier and level
4. Select quest to view details (objectives, rewards)
5. Accept quest to begin tracking progress

### Tracking Progress

Players can track their active quests and their progress through the in-game Quest Log. Objectives show their completion status, and progress persists across game sessions.

### Completing Quests

1. Complete all quest objectives
2. Return to a Quest Block
3. Interact to claim rewards
4. Receive XP and items immediately
5. Quest marked as completed (cannot repeat)

### Quest Limits

The number of active quests a player can have simultaneously is dynamic and depends on their overall quest progression:
- **2 active quests:** Initially (Novice tier)
- **3 active quests:** After completing 5 quests (Apprentice tier)
- **5 active quests:** After completing 10 quests (Expert tier)
- **7 active quests:** After completing 20 quests (Master tier)

### Class and Race Specific Quests

Some quests are designed specifically for certain classes or races. These quests will only be available to players who meet the class or race requirements, offering unique storylines and rewards tailored to their character.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How many total quests are there?**
A: 64 total quests - 40 race-specific (10 per race) + 24 general quests

**Q: Can I repeat quests after completing them?**
A: Generally no, quests can only be completed once per character. However, some quests may be marked as repeatable (e.g., Daily Quests).

**Q: What happens if I lose items before turning in a fetch quest?**
A: You'll need to gather the items again. Items must be in your inventory when claiming rewards.

**Q: Do environmental kills count for kill quests?**
A: No, only direct player kills count toward quest progress.

**Q: Can I skip quest tiers?**
A: No, you must meet level requirements. Novice (1) ‚Üí Apprentice (5) ‚Üí Expert (15) ‚Üí Master (25).

**Q: Can I do quest chains for races other than my own?**
A: No, you can only complete the quest chain for your selected race.

**Q: How do I check my quest progress?**
A: You can check your active quests and their progress through the in-game Quest Log.

**Q: Are there any quests that require specific classes?**
A: Yes, there are class-specific quests (categorized as 'CLASS') in addition to the race-specific quest chains.
