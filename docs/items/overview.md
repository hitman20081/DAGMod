## Item Storage & Management Tips

**Class Ability Items:**
- Keep in hotbar for quick access
- Lost on death, following standard Minecraft death rules
- Essential for combat

**Consumable Powders:**
- Store extras in base
- Bring what you need for specific situations
- One-time use - use wisely

**Armor Sets:**
- Wear complete sets for bonuses
- Store backup sets
- Progressive power - keep upgrading

**Spell Scrolls:**
- Store extras in base
- Bring what you need for challenges
- Cannot be recrafted - use strategically

**Foods:**
- Stack premium foods for boss fights
- Keep basic foods for general use
- Store extras in base

**Tokens:**
- Used immediately on selection
- Not applicable after character creation

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How do I get the 17 new class ability items?**
A: Class ability items are primarily obtained through quest rewards and crafting.

**Q: Can I trade items with other players?**
A: Most items can be traded except selection tokens and bound items.

**Q: What happens to my items when I die?**
A: Items follow standard Minecraft death rules. Class ability items are lost on death.

**Q: Can I craft spell scrolls?**
A: As of v1.5.0-beta, spell scrolls cannot be crafted. Obtain through Mage quests.

**Q: How do armor set bonuses work?**
A: Armor sets provide progressive bonuses. A 2-piece bonus activates when two pieces of the same set are worn, and a more powerful 4-piece bonus activates when a full set is worn. These bonuses are detailed in the [Armor Sets](<./ARMOR_SETS.md>) section.
